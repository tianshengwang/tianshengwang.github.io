Postdoctoral Fellowship in Causal Inference & Dementia Research

The CausalRx.AI Lab in the Population Health Outcomes and Pharmacoepidemiology Education and Research Center (P-HOPER Center) and the Department of Pharmaceutical Health Outcomes and Policy (PHOP) at the University of Houston College of Pharmacy invites applications for a full-time postdoctoral fellow focused on the development and application of methods for estimating the causal effects of treatment strategies on Alzheimer‚Äôs disease and related dementias (ADRD).

The mission of the P-HOPER Center is to conduct innovative population health outcomes research and offer educational programs and population practices that impact the region and the nation. The University of Houston is a Carnegie-designated Tier One public research university and a member of the world-renowned Texas Medical Center, which houses major clinical and academic programs. With over 40,000 students, UH is one of the most culturally and ethnically diverse public universities in the United States.

This fellowship offers a unique opportunity to work with complex, real-world data and modern causal inference methodology to inform clinical decision-making in dementia care. Current projects focus on evaluating the comparative effectiveness of drug repurposing candidates and FDA-approved monoclonal antibody therapies on dementia outcomes. The fellow will contribute to ongoing drug repurposing pipelines, pursue independent dementia-focused research interests, and develop their own research program. Work will involve large-scale analyses of observational electronic health records (EHR) and locally sourced clinical datasets.

The position is for up to two years with competitive compensation and the potential for renewal based on performance and funding availability. Funds will be available for travel to present research and to build collaborations in the field.

Primary Responsibilities

  • Conduct advanced analyses using large-scale EHR and administrative claims data (e.g., Medicare).
  • Lead manuscript preparation and present findings at national and international conferences.
  • Collaborate with investigators across neuroscience, (pharmaco)epidemiology, biostatistics, and computer science.
  • Optional: mentor junior trainees and contribute to grant development.

Required Qualifications

  • Ph.D. in Pharmaceutical Outcomes, (Pharmaco)epidemiology, Biostatistics, Computer Science, Bioinformatics, Health Informatics, or a related field.
  • Expertise in‚Äîor strong interest in learning‚Äîmachine learning and deep learning algorithms.
  • Proficiency in Python and familiarity with relevant libraries (e.g., PyTorch).
  • Excellent communication skills and ability to work collaboratively in a multidisciplinary environment.
