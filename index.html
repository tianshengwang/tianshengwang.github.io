Research Focus

I develop and apply AI‑powered causal inference methods to assess drug effects in real‑world data. Current themes:

  • Heterogeneous Treatment Effects (HTE) assessment: heterogeneous causal effects assessment and subgroup identification in real‑world data.
  • Confounding Control: Longitudinal patterns (e.g., thdPS) of features for confounding control (collaborative project with Dr. Til Stürmer @ UNC).
  • Drug repurposing for Alzheimer's disease and related dementia (ADRD): assess causal effects of drug repurposing candidates on dementia.

Active Research Areas

HTE Assessment

Using Iterative Causal Forest (iCF) and High‑dimensional iCF (hdiCF) to detect and characterize heterogeneous treatment effects in observational data.

  • Iterative Causal Forest — Am J Epidemiol, 2024
  • High‑dimensional iCF (hdiCF) — Am J Epidemiol, 2024
  • High‑dimensional HTE in Claims — Am J Epidemiol, 2025
Figure: iCF / hdiCF workflow (placeholder)

Confounding Control

Temporal high‑dimensional propensity score (thdPS), proximal causal inference, and target trial emulation for bias reduction under complex treatment dynamics.

  • Handling Switchers in Treatment Effect Estimation — Am J Epidemiol, 2025
Figure: thdPS timeline patterns (placeholder)

Drug Repurposing for ADRD

Evaluating candidate drugs for Alzheimer's disease and related dementias with claims‑based phenotyping and robust causal ML pipelines.

  • Claims‑based Dementia Algorithm (ARIC/Medicare) — Am J Epidemiol, 2025
Figure: ADRD identification algorithm (placeholder)

Methods Development

Designing novel high‑dimensional settings (e.g., one‑ordinal‑per‑code) and learning algorithms for reliable subgroup discovery and effect estimation.

  • HDsetting — code base
  • hdiCF — implementation
